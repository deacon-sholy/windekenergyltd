The Energy Sector Crisis

Key structural bottlenecks currently hindering development and driving energy poverty in Nigeria.

01

Reliance on Biomass & Fossil Fuels

Heavy reliance on firewood and charcoal causes severe deforestation and environmental health hazards, contributing to respiratory illnesses among women and children.

02

High Distribution & Supply Costs

Massive logistical inefficiencies, import congestions, and demurrage penalties drive LPG retail prices up, pricing clean options out of reach for half the population.

03

Lack of Storage Infrastructure

Domestic storage is severely constrained. While supply is available, the absence of bulk terminals creates a structural bottleneck preventing market penetration.

04

High Grid T&D Inefficiencies

The electrical grid experiences massive losses (~30% transmission and distribution losses) and insufficient generation capacity (only ~10,396 MW installed).

05

Energy Deficits & Import Dependencies

Persistent energy deficits force local markets to remain exposed to foreign supply-chain shocks, currency fluctuations, and localized shortages.

50% - 70%

Environmental & Infrastructural Concerns

Environmental challenges and outdated generation infrastructure collectively account for 50%-70% of the total systemic failures in Nigeria's power and energy supply chain.

Source: KPMG Nigeria Power Sector Guide 2023 | Detail Solicitors 2024

Global Ingestion & Infrastructure Models

Our operational blueprints are modeled after highly successful global government and private sector transitions:

India: PMUY (Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana)

Over 90 million households successfully transitioned from wood fuels to LPG. This massive growth was supported by constructing 11,000+ new distributors and bottling plants, significantly improving public health and reducing indoor pollution.

Ghana: CRM (Cylinder Recirculation Model)

A national policy that increased LPG penetration from 25% to 50% by building massive storage infrastructure, optimizing distributor safety, and centralizing cylinder bottling.
